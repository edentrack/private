# Edentrack Farm Management App - Complete Guide for Teens 🐔📱

## What is Edentrack? 

Imagine you have a chicken farm - you've got hundreds of chickens, and you need to keep track of EVERYTHING. How many chickens do you have? How much feed did you buy? Did you sell any eggs today? Which chickens got their vaccines? Who's working today?

**Edentrack is like a super-smart digital notebook** that remembers everything about your farm. Instead of writing things down on paper (which you could lose!), you enter everything into this app on your phone or computer, and it does all the math and tracking for you!

---

## 🎯 The Main Goal

The goal is simple: **Keep track of everything on your farm so you can make money and grow your business!**

When you know:
- How much you're spending (expenses)
- How much you're earning (sales)
- How your chickens are growing (health & weight)
- What tasks need to be done today

...you can make better decisions and run a successful farm!

## 📱 Getting Started - Your First Steps

### Step 1: Create Your Account
- Open the Edentrack app
- Click **"Sign Up"** 
- Enter your **email** and create a **password**
- Enter your **full name** and basic information
- Click **"Create Account"**
- You're now the **Owner** of your farm! 🎉

### Step 2: Set Up Your Farm Profile
- Go to **Settings** (the gear icon ⚙️ in the top right or bottom navigation)
- Click on **"Farm Information"**
- Enter your **farm name** (like "Joe's Chicken Farm" or "Mama's Poultry")
- Choose your **country** from the dropdown (this helps the app set the right currency)
- The app automatically sets your **currency**:
  - Cameroon = XAF (Central African Franc)
  - Nigeria = NGN (Naira)
  - Ghana = GHS (Cedis)
  - Kenya = KES (Shillings)
  - And more!
- Click **"Save Settings"**

### Step 3: Create Your First Flock
A **flock** = a group of chickens that arrived on the same day and are the same type (broiler or layer).

- Click **"Flocks"** in the top navigation menu
- Click the big green **"Add New Flock"** button
- Fill in the form:
  - **Flock Name**: Give it a memorable name like "Broiler Batch 1" or "Layer Flock A"
  - **Type**: Choose either:
    - **Broiler** = Chickens for meat (you'll sell them whole after 6-8 weeks)
    - **Layer** = Chickens for eggs (they'll lay eggs for about 72 weeks/1.5 years)
  - **Breed** (optional): Like Cobb 500, Ross 308, Lohmann Brown, etc.
  - **Initial Count**: How many chickens do you have? (like 500, 1000, etc.)
  - **Arrival Date**: When did these chickens arrive? (Today's date, or whenever they came)
  - **House/Pen Name** (optional): Which building or area are they in? (like "House 1" or "Pen A")
- Click **"Save"** or **"Create Flock"**

🎉 **Congratulations!** You've created your first flock. Now the app will track everything about this group of chickens - their age, weight, health, expenses, and sales!

## 🏠 Main Features - What You Can Do (Explained Simply)

### 1. 📊 **Dashboard** - Your Farm's Control Center

Think of the Dashboard as the **main screen** - like the home page on your phone. It shows you everything important at a glance!

**What You'll See at the Top:**
- **Total Birds**: How many chickens you have in total across ALL flocks (e.g., "1,500 birds")
- **Active Flocks**: How many different groups of chickens you're managing (e.g., "3 flocks")
- **Pending Tasks**: How many things need to be done today (e.g., "5 tasks")
- **Current Week**: How old your selected flock is in weeks (e.g., "Week 3" means they're 3 weeks old)

**The Progress Bar:**
This colorful bar shows you how far through the lifecycle your chickens are.
- For **Broilers**: Usually 6-8 weeks until they're ready to sell
  - Week 1-2 = Baby chicks (Brooding phase)
  - Week 3-4 = Growing fast (Growth phase)
  - Week 5-8 = Almost ready to sell (Finishing phase)
- For **Layers**: Usually 72 weeks (about 1.5 years) until they stop laying eggs
  - Week 1-5 = Baby chicks (Chick phase)
  - Week 6-12 = Growing up (Grower phase)
  - Week 13-17 = Almost adults (Pullet phase)
  - Week 18-20 = About to start laying (Pre-lay phase)
  - Week 21-72 = Laying eggs! (Laying phase)

**Quick Action Buttons:**
- **Record Sale**: Sell chickens or eggs quickly without going to a different page
- **Manage Stock**: Quickly check and update your inventory

**Widgets (Little Boxes of Information):**

1. **Today's Tasks Widget**: 
   - Shows tasks that need to be done TODAY
   - Click a task to mark it complete
   - Add new tasks quickly

2. **Quick Egg Collection Widget** (for layer flocks):
   - Record eggs you collected today
   - Enter by size (small/medium/large/jumbo)
   - One click and it's saved!

3. **Production Cycle Widget**:
   - Shows which phase your chickens are in (Baby → Growing → Adult)
   - Visual timeline showing where you are in the cycle
   - Shows current phase name (like "Growth" or "Laying")

4. **Weight Progress Widget**:
   - Shows a graph of how your chickens are growing
   - Compares actual weight vs. target weight
   - Tells you if they're growing well or too slowly

5. **Inventory Usage Widget**:
   - **FASTEST** way to record daily feed usage
   - Select item from dropdown (like "Starter Feed")
   - Enter quantity used today
   - Click "Record" - done in 10 seconds!

**Why the Dashboard is Useful:**
Instead of clicking through 10 different pages to see what's happening, you see everything important in ONE place. It's like having a TV dashboard in your car showing speed, fuel, temperature - all at once!

### 2. 🐔 **Flocks** - Managing Your Chicken Groups

**What is a Flock?**
A flock is a **group of chickens** that:
- Arrived on the **same day**
- Are the **same type** (all broilers OR all layers)
- Are usually the **same breed**
- Live in the same **house or pen**

You DON'T track each chicken individually (that would be too much!). Instead, you track **groups**.

**Example:**
- "Broiler Batch 1" = 500 chickens that all came on January 1st
- "Layer Flock A" = 300 chickens that all came on March 15th

**What You Can Do on the Flocks Page:**

1. **View All Your Flocks**:
   - See every group of chickens you have
   - See key info: Number of birds alive, age, type (broiler/layer)
   - See status: Active, Archived, or Sold

2. **Filter Flocks**:
   - Show only **Broilers** (meat chickens)
   - Show only **Layers** (egg chickens)
   - Show only **Active** flocks (currently on your farm)
   - Show **Archived** flocks (sold or removed)

3. **Search**:
   - Type a flock name to find it quickly

4. **Create New Flock**:
   - Click **"Add New Flock"**
   - Fill in all the details (see Step 3 above)
   - Save and start tracking!

**Important Numbers on Each Flock Card:**

- **Initial Count**: How many you started with (never changes)
  - Example: Started with 500 birds
- **Current Count**: How many are still alive (decreases when birds die or are sold)
  - Example: Started with 500, but 10 died and 100 were sold, so Current Count = 390
- **Age**: Automatically calculated from arrival date
  - Example: If they arrived 3 weeks ago, Age = 3 weeks

**Archiving a Flock:**
When you sell all the chickens or remove a flock:
- Click **"Archive Flock"**
- Archived flocks stay in your records (for reports and history)
- But they won't show in your main list anymore (keeps things clean!)

### 3. 💰 **Expenses** - Tracking What You Spend Money On

**Why Track Expenses?**
To know if you're making **profit**, you need to know how much you're **spending**!

**Simple Math:**
- **Expenses** = Money going OUT (spending) 💸
- **Sales/Revenue** = Money coming IN (earning) 💵
- **Profit** = Sales - Expenses
  - If Sales > Expenses = You're making money! ✅
  - If Sales < Expenses = You're losing money! ❌

**What Counts as an Expense?**
- **Feed**: Buying chicken feed (usually your biggest expense!)
  - Starter feed, Grower feed, Layer feed, etc.
- **Medication**: Vaccines, antibiotics, vitamins, supplements
- **Labor**: Paying workers (salary, wages)
- **Equipment**: Heaters, feeders, waterers, nets, etc.
- **Utilities**: Electricity bills, water bills
- **Transport**: Moving chickens or supplies (gas, vehicle costs)
- **Veterinary**: Vet visits and consultations
- **Miscellaneous**: Anything else you buy for the farm

**How to Record an Expense:**

1. Go to **Expenses** page (click "$ Expenses" in the navigation)
2. Click the green **"Add Expense"** button
3. Fill in the form:
   - **Amount**: How much did you spend? 
     - Example: 150,000 XAF
   - **Description**: What did you buy?
     - Example: "Starter Feed - 50 bags"
   - **Category**: Choose from the list:
     - Feed, Medication, Labor, Equipment, Utilities, Transport, Veterinary, Miscellaneous
   - **Date**: When did you spend it? (usually today)
   - **Flock** (optional): Is this expense for a specific flock?
     - Example: Buying feed just for "Broiler Batch 1"
   - **Link to Inventory** (optional but SMART!):
     - If you bought feed, check this box!
     - The app will automatically add the feed to your inventory
     - You don't have to enter it twice!
   - **Photo** (optional): Take a photo of the receipt
     - This is like having proof if you need it later!
4. Click **"Save"**

**Smart Features:**

1. **Link to Inventory**:
   - When you buy feed, link it to inventory
   - The app adds the feed to your stock automatically
   - Saves you time (don't have to enter it twice!)

2. **Reports**:
   - See **Total Expenses** this month, this week, this year, or all time
   - See expenses **per flock** (how much did "Broiler Batch 1" cost?)
   - See expenses **by category** (how much did you spend on feed vs. medication?)

3. **Trends**:
   - See if your expenses are increasing or decreasing
   - Identify what you spend most on (probably feed!)

### 4. 💵 **Sales** - Tracking What You Earn

This is the FUN part - tracking money coming IN! 💰

**Two Types of Sales:**

#### A. **Bird Sales** (Selling Whole Chickens)
Perfect for broiler farms! You raise chickens for 6-8 weeks, then sell them whole.

**How to Record a Bird Sale:**

1. Go to **Sales** page (click "$ Sales" in navigation)
2. Click **"Bird Sales"** tab (at the top)
3. Click the green **"Record Sale"** button
4. Fill in the form:

   **Step 1: Choose Flock**
   - Select which flock you're selling from
   - Example: "Broiler Batch 1"
   - See how many birds are available (can't sell more than you have!)

   **Step 2: Find or Add Customer**
   - Enter their **phone number** in the search box
   - **If they've bought before**: Their name and info fills automatically! 🎉
   - **If new customer**: Enter their name and phone number
   - The app saves customers automatically for next time!

   **Step 3: Enter Sale Details**
   - **Quantity**: How many birds sold?
     - Example: 50 birds
   - **Selling Method**: Choose one:
     - **Per Bird**: Fixed price per chicken
       - Example: 3,000 XAF per bird
       - Total = 50 × 3,000 = 150,000 XAF
     - **Per Kg**: Price based on weight
       - Example: 2,500 XAF per kg
       - If birds weigh 2kg each, and you sold 50 birds:
       - Total weight = 50 × 2 = 100 kg
       - Total = 100 × 2,500 = 250,000 XAF
   - **Price per Bird** or **Price per Kg**: Enter the price
   - **Weight** (if selling per kg): Enter total weight in kg
   - **Total Amount**: App calculates automatically, or enter manually

   **Step 4: Payment Information**
   - **Payment Status**: 
     - **Paid**: Customer paid now (cash, mobile money, etc.)
     - **Pending**: Customer will pay later
   - **Payment Method**:
     - Cash
     - Card (credit/debit card)
     - Mobile Money (MTN, Orange Money, etc.)
     - Bank Transfer

   **Step 5: Notes (Optional)**
   - Add any special details
   - Example: "Customer requested delivery on Saturday"

5. Click **"Save"** or **"Record Sale"**

**What Happens Automatically:**
- Your flock's **Current Count** decreases (those 50 birds are removed from your flock)
- The sale is recorded in your **Revenue** (money earned)
- Customer info is **saved** for next time (super convenient!)
- You can **print or send a receipt** via WhatsApp

#### B. **Egg Sales** (Selling Eggs)
Perfect for layer farms! You collect eggs daily, then sell them.

**How to Record an Egg Sale:**

1. Go to **Sales** page
2. Click **"Egg Sales"** tab
3. Click **"Record Sale"** button
4. Fill in the form:

   **Step 1: Find or Add Customer**
   - Search by phone number (same as bird sales)
   - Customer info auto-fills if they've bought before!

   **Step 2: Enter Eggs by Size**
   - **Small Eggs**: How many?
   - **Medium Eggs**: How many?
   - **Large Eggs**: How many?
   - **Jumbo Eggs**: How many?
   - **OR sell by Trays**: Each tray = 30 eggs typically
   - The app calculates **Total Eggs** automatically

   **Step 3: Enter Prices**
   - **Price per Egg**: How much per egg? (or price per tray)
   - Example: 100 XAF per large egg
   - App calculates **Total Amount** automatically

   **Step 4: Payment Information**
   - Choose **Payment Method** and **Payment Status**

5. Click **"Save"**

**What Happens:**
- Eggs are **deducted from your inventory** automatically
- Sale is recorded as **revenue**
- Customer is **saved** for future sales
- You can print or send receipt

**Customer Management:**
- All customers are saved automatically
- View all customers in **"Customer List"**
- Edit customer information anytime
- See sales history per customer

### 5. 📦 **Inventory** - Keeping Track of What You Have

**What is Inventory?**
Inventory = All the stuff you have in storage:
- Feed (starter, grower, layer, etc.)
- Medication (vaccines, antibiotics, vitamins)
- Equipment (heaters, feeders, waterers)
- Supplies (bedding, cleaning supplies)

**Why Track Inventory?**
- Know what you have (so you don't run out!)
- Know when to buy more (before you run out!)
- Track how much you're using (cost tracking)
- Get alerts when stock is low

**How Inventory Works:**

**Step 1: Add Items to Inventory**
When you first get something (or buy something):

1. Go to **Inventory** page
2. Choose the **category**: Feed, Medication, Equipment, or Supplies
3. Click **"Add"** button
4. Fill in:
   - **Name**: "Starter Feed Premium" or "Newcastle Vaccine"
   - **Unit**: How do you measure it?
     - Feed: **Bag** or **Kg**
     - Medication: **Bottle**, **Vial**, **Liter**
     - Equipment: **Each**, **Set**
     - Supplies: **Pack**, **Roll**, etc.
   - **Current Stock**: How many do you have right now?
     - Example: 50 bags, or 20 bottles
   - **Cost per Unit**: How much does each unit cost?
     - Example: 3,000 XAF per bag
   - **Supplier** (optional): Where did you buy it?
5. Click **"Save"**

**Step 2: Record Usage (When You Use Something)**
Every day when you use feed, medication, or supplies:

**Method 1: Quick Way (Dashboard Widget)**
1. On Dashboard, find **"Inventory Usage"** widget
2. Click dropdown and select item (like "Starter Feed")
3. Enter quantity used (like "5 bags")
4. Click **"Record"**
5. Done in 10 seconds! ⚡

**Method 2: From Inventory Page**
1. Go to **Inventory** page
2. Find the item in the list
3. Click **"Record Usage"** button
4. Enter:
   - **Quantity Used**: How much did you use?
     - Example: 5 bags of starter feed
   - **Date**: When did you use it? (usually today)
   - **Flock** (optional): Which flock did you use it for?
     - Example: Used for "Broiler Batch 1"
   - **Notes**: Why did you use it?
     - Example: "Daily morning feeding"
5. Click **"Save"**

**What Happens:**
- Your **Stock Level** decreases automatically
  - If you had 50 bags, and used 5, now you have 45 bags
- The app tracks your **usage history**
  - See how much you used each day
  - See trends (are you using more or less?)

**Smart Alerts:**
The app watches your inventory and tells you when it's getting low!

- **Empty Alert**: "⚠️ You're out of Starter Feed! Add more now."
- **Critical Low Alert**: "🔴 Only 2 bags left! Less than 1 day remaining. Restock soon!"
- **Running Low Alert**: "🟡 10 bags left. 3 days remaining. Consider restocking."

These alerts appear:
- On the Dashboard
- In the Notifications bell icon (red badge shows number of alerts)
- In the Alerts sidebar

**Inventory Categories:**

1. **Feed**:
   - Starter Feed (for baby chicks, 0-3 weeks)
   - Grower Feed (for growing chickens, 3-6 weeks)
   - Layer Feed (for laying hens)
   - Finisher Feed (for broilers near market weight)

2. **Medication**:
   - Vaccines (Newcastle, Gumboro, etc.)
   - Antibiotics (for treating sick birds)
   - Vitamins and supplements
   - Deworming medication

3. **Equipment**:
   - Heaters (brooders for baby chicks)
   - Feeders (containers for feed)
   - Waterers (containers for water)
   - Nets, cages, etc.

4. **Supplies**:
   - Bedding material (wood shavings, etc.)
   - Cleaning supplies
   - Disinfectants

**Pro Tip**: When you buy feed or medication, use the "Link to Inventory" option when recording the expense. The app automatically adds it to your inventory - saves time!

### 6. 📋 **Tasks** - What Needs to Be Done Today

**Why Use Tasks?**
Instead of trying to remember "feed chickens at 7am, collect eggs at 8am, check water at 9am, clean coop at 2pm," you create tasks and assign them to workers!

**Two Types of Tasks:**

#### A. **One-Time Tasks** (Just for Today)
Examples:
- "Feed Broiler Batch 1 at 7am"
- "Collect eggs from Layer Flock A"
- "Give Newcastle vaccine to Flock B"
- "Clean House 1"

#### B. **Recurring Tasks** (Automatic - Created Daily/Weekly)
Examples:
- "Daily feeding at 7am" (creates automatically every day!)
- "Weekly weight check every Monday"
- "Monthly cleaning on the 1st"

**Creating a Regular Task:**

1. Go to **Tasks** page
2. Click **"Add Task"** button
3. Fill in:
   - **Title**: "Feed Flock A"
   - **Description**: "Give 5 bags of starter feed" (optional but helpful)
   - **Due Date**: Today, tomorrow, or any future date
   - **Due Time**: What time? (like 7:00 AM)
   - **Assign To**: 
     - Yourself (if you're doing it)
     - Or a worker (if someone else is doing it)
   - **Flock** (optional): Which flock is this for?
     - Example: "Broiler Batch 1"
4. Click **"Save"**

**Setting Up Task Templates (Automatic Tasks):**

Instead of creating "Feed chickens at 7am" every single day, set it up once and the app creates it automatically!

1. Go to **Settings** → **Task Templates**
2. Click **"Add Template"**
3. Fill in:
   - **Title**: "Daily Morning Feeding"
   - **Description**: "Feed all flocks starter/grower feed"
   - **Frequency**: 
     - **Daily**: Every day at the same time
     - **Weekly**: Every week on specific days (Monday, Wednesday, Friday, etc.)
     - **Monthly**: Same date every month (like 1st of every month)
   - **Time**: What time? (7:00 AM)
   - **Applies To**: Which flocks?
     - All flocks, or specific ones
4. Click **"Save"**

**Now What?**
The app automatically creates this task every day at 7am! You don't have to do anything - it just appears in your task list. Magic! ✨

**Completing Tasks:**

**For Workers:**
1. Open the **Tasks** page (or Dashboard widget)
2. See tasks assigned to you
3. Click a task to open it
4. Click **"Mark Complete"** or **"Complete"** button
5. Add **Notes** (optional): "Fed 5 bags, all chickens ate well"
6. Add **Photos** (optional): Take photo as proof
7. Click **"Save"**

**For Owners/Managers:**
- You can complete tasks yourself
- Or check tasks completed by workers
- View completion notes and photos

**Task Status:**
- **Pending**: Not done yet (still to do)
- **Completed**: Done! ✅
- **Overdue**: Past due date and still not done (red color, needs attention!)

**Viewing Tasks:**
- **Today's Tasks**: See what needs to be done TODAY
- **Upcoming Tasks**: See tasks for future dates
- **Overdue Tasks**: See tasks that are past due
- **Completed Tasks**: See what's already done (in Task History page)

### 7. 👥 **Team** - Managing Your Workers

**Why Have a Team?**
You can't do everything alone! Workers help with:
- Feeding chickens
- Collecting eggs
- Cleaning coops
- Giving medications
- Completing daily tasks

**Roles (What Each Person Can Do):**

1. **Owner** (That's You!):
   - Full access to EVERYTHING
   - Can manage team, billing, settings
   - Can create, edit, delete everything

2. **Manager**:
   - Almost everything (like Owner)
   - Can manage farm operations
   - Can view financials, create expenses, manage payroll
   - **CANNOT**: Change billing, promote others to Owner

3. **Worker**:
   - Can complete tasks assigned to them
   - Can view basic flock info (but NOT costs/prices)
   - Can record usage, collect eggs, log mortality
   - **CANNOT**: See expenses, sales, payroll, or costs

4. **Viewer**:
   - Can ONLY look at data (read-only)
   - Cannot change anything
   - Good for investors or advisors who just want to see reports

**Inviting Team Members:**

1. Go to **Team** page
2. Click **"Invite Member"** button
3. Enter their **Email** or **Phone Number**
4. Choose their **Role**: Manager, Worker, or Viewer
5. Click **"Send Invitation"**

**What Happens:**
- They receive an invitation link (via email or WhatsApp)
- They click the link
- They create an account (or sign in if they already have one)
- They automatically join your farm!
- They can now access the app with their role's permissions

**Managing Team Members:**

**Set Pay Rates:**
- For each worker, set their hourly rate or salary
- Example: 2,000 XAF per hour, or 100,000 XAF per month
- This is used for payroll calculation

**Change Roles:**
- Promote a worker to manager (if they're doing well!)
- Demote a manager to worker (if needed)
- Only Owners can change roles

**Deactivate Member:**
- Remove someone from your team
- They lose access to your farm
- But you keep all their records and history

**View Activity:**
- See what each team member has been doing
- Track who completed which tasks
- Monitor changes made to the farm

**Team Activity Log:**
- See a history of all team actions
- Who added a flock, who recorded a sale, who completed a task
- Helps you track what's happening on your farm
- Automatically deletes old logs (after 1 week) to keep things fast

### 8. 💼 **Payroll** - Paying Your Workers

**How Payroll Works (Step-by-Step):**

**Step 1: Set Up Worker Pay Rates**
- Go to **Team** page
- For each worker, click **"Set Pay"** or **"Set Compensation"**
- Choose:
  - **Hourly**: Paid per hour worked (like 2,000 XAF/hour)
  - **Salary**: Fixed amount per month (like 100,000 XAF/month)
- Enter the amount
- Click **"Save"**

**Step 2: Record Worker Shifts**
- Workers work shifts (specific times)
- See "Shifts" section below for how to create shifts
- The app tracks which shifts each worker worked
- Calculates hours automatically

**Step 3: Create Payroll Run**
At the end of pay period (weekly/monthly):

1. Go to **Payroll** page
2. Click **"Create Pay Run"** or **"Process Payroll"**
3. Select **Pay Period**:
   - Start Date: When does this pay period start?
   - End Date: When does it end?
   - Example: January 1 - January 31 (monthly)
4. Click **"Generate Preview"**

**Step 4: Review Payroll**
The app shows you:
- **Workers**: List of all workers who worked during this period
- **Hours Worked**: Calculated from their shifts
- **Base Pay**: Hours × Hourly Rate (or fixed salary)
- **Overtime (OT)**: Extra hours (if they worked more than normal)
- **Bonuses**: Extra money you want to give (optional)
- **Deductions**: Money to subtract (if they borrowed money, etc.)
- **Net Pay**: Final amount they'll receive (Base + OT + Bonuses - Deductions)

**Step 5: Adjust if Needed**
- Add bonuses (good work bonus, etc.)
- Add deductions (if worker borrowed money, etc.)
- Review everything looks correct

**Step 6: Process Payroll**
- Click **"Process Payroll"**
- Confirm the amounts
- Click **"Process"** or **"Approve"**

**What Happens:**
- **Pay Stubs** are created automatically
- Workers can view their pay stubs
- The payroll is recorded as an **Expense** automatically (so it shows in your expenses!)
- Payment history is saved

**Pay Stubs:**
- Workers can view their pay stubs in the **"Pay Stubs"** tab
- Shows: Hours worked, gross pay, deductions, net pay
- Can be exported or printed

**Payroll Adjustments:**
- Add bonuses after payroll is processed
- Add deductions
- Make corrections if needed

For special occasions or one-time work

   - **End Time**: When do they finish?
5. Click **"Save"**

**Views:**

1. **Today & Tomorrow View**:
   - See shifts happening soon (today and tomorrow)

2. **Calendar View**:
   - See all shifts in a monthly calendar
   - Click a date to see shifts for that day
   - Visual overview of who's working when

**How Shifts Help:**
- Workers know when to come to work
- You know who's working each day

### 10. 💉 **Vaccinations** - Health Management

**Why Vaccinations are Important:**
Vaccines prevent diseases that could kill ALL your chickens! Without vaccines, one sick chicken could spread disease to everyone.

### 12. ☠️ **Mortality** - Tracking Deaths

**Why Track Mortality (Deaths)?**
- Know your **survival rate** (how many lived vs died)
- Identify problems early (if many are dying, something's wrong!)
- Update your bird count accurately (so your numbers are correct)
- Track causes of death (disease? Heat stress? Accident?)

**Recording Mortality:**

1. Go to **Mortality** page
   - OR click **"Log Mortality"** button from the Flocks page
2. Click **"Log Mortality"** or **"Record Death"** button
3. Fill in:
   - **Select Flock**: Which flock lost birds?
   - **Number of Birds**: How many died?
     - Example: 5 birds
   - **Date**: When did they die? (usually today, or when you discovered them)
   - **Cause**: Why did they die?
     - **Disease**: Sick with disease
     - **Heat Stress**: Too hot (common in hot weather)

     - **Accident**: Crushed, attacked, etc.

     - **Other**: Enter custom cause

**What Happens:**

**Mortality Rate:**
- **Low Mortality Rate** (less than 1-2%) = Normal, healthy flock ✅
- **High Mortality Rate** (more than 5%) = Problem! ⚠️

  - Check temperature (too hot or too cold?)

### 14. 📈 **Insights & Analytics** - Understanding Your Farm's Performance

**What is Analytics?**
Analytics = Looking at all your data to understand:
- Are you making profit?
- Are your chickens growing well?
- What are you spending most on?
- How efficient are you?

**Why It's Important:**
Data helps you make **smart decisions**:
- "Am I making money?" → Check Net Profit
- "Should I buy more feed?" → Check inventory and usage trends
- "Are my chickens growing well?" → Check Feed Conversion Ratio
- "When should I sell?" → Check Market Ready status

**What You Can See:**

#### **Financial Overview:**
- **Total Expenses**: How much you've spent
  - This week, this month, this year, or all time
  - Example: "150,000 XAF this week"
- **Total Revenue**: How much you've earned (from sales)
  - Example: "200,000 XAF this week"
- **Net Profit**: Revenue - Expenses = Profit!
  - Example: 200,000 - 150,000 = **50,000 XAF profit** ✅
  - If positive = Making money!
  - If negative = Losing money (need to reduce expenses or increase sales!)
- **Profit Margin**: What % of revenue is profit?
  - Example: 50,000 profit ÷ 200,000 revenue = 25% profit margin
  - Higher = Better!

#### **Production Metrics:**
- **Current Age**: How old are your flocks? (in weeks)
- **Birds Alive**: How many chickens do you currently have?
- **Mortality Rate**: What % died? (Lower is better!)
- **Feed Consumed**: How much feed did you use? (in bags or kg)
- **Eggs Collected**: How many eggs did you collect? (for layers)

#### **Efficiency Metrics:**
- **Feed Conversion Ratio (FCR)**: 
  - How much feed per kg of weight gain?
  - Example: 2.0 FCR = Used 2kg of feed to get 1kg of chicken weight
  - **Lower FCR = Better** (means chickens are growing efficiently!)
  - Good FCR for broilers: 1.5-2.0
- **Cost Efficiency**:
  - Cost per bird
  - Lower = Better (means you're spending less per chicken)
- **Survival Rate**:
  - What % of birds survived?
  - Higher = Better!
  - Example: 98% survival = Very good! Only 2% died
- **Daily Average Cost**:
  - How much are you spending per day?
  - Helps with budgeting

#### **Week-by-Week Breakdown:**
- See expenses, revenue, and profit for each week
- Compare this week to last week
- Identify trends (are costs increasing? Are sales increasing?)

**How to Use Analytics:**

1. **Check Weekly**:
   - Every week, go to Insights page
   - See if you're making profit
   - Check if expenses are too high
   - Check if production is good

2. **Compare Flocks**:
   - See which flocks are performing best
   - Learn from successful flocks
   - Fix problems in struggling flocks

3. **Export Reports**:
   - Export as PDF or Excel
   - Share with investors, advisors, or accountants
   - Keep for your records

4. **Make Decisions**:
   - "Expenses are too high" → Reduce unnecessary spending
   - "Production is low" → Check feed, health, environment
   - "Profit is good" → Consider expanding!

## 🎓 Key Concepts Explained Simply

### **Flock vs Individual Birds**
- You don't track each chicken individually (that would be too much work!)
- Instead, you track **groups** (flocks) that arrived together
- Example: "Broiler Batch 1" = 500 chickens that all came on the same day
- You track the GROUP, not each individual bird

### **Broiler vs Layer**
- **Broiler** = Chickens raised for **meat**
  - You'll sell them whole after 6-8 weeks
  - They grow fast and get fat quickly
  - Example: Cobb 500, Ross 308 breeds
- **Layer** = Chickens raised for **eggs**
  - They lay eggs for about 72 weeks (1.5 years)
  - Then you might sell them too
  - Example: Lohmann Brown, ISA Brown breeds

### **Expenses vs Sales vs Profit**
- **Expenses** = Money going OUT 💸 (you're spending)
  - Buying feed, paying workers, buying medication
- **Sales/Revenue** = Money coming IN 💵 (you're earning)
  - Selling chickens, selling eggs
- **Profit** = Sales - Expenses
  - Example: Earned 200,000 XAF, Spent 150,000 XAF
  - Profit = 200,000 - 150,000 = **50,000 XAF** ✅

### **Current Count vs Initial Count**
- **Initial Count** = How many chickens you started with (never changes)
  - Example: Started with 500 birds
- **Current Count** = How many are still alive (decreases when birds die or are sold)
  - Example: Started with 500
  - 10 died (mortality)
  - 100 were sold
  - Current Count = 500 - 10 - 100 = **390 birds**

### **Inventory: Stock vs Usage**
- **Stock** = How much you HAVE right now (like "I have 50 bags of feed")
- **Usage** = How much you USE (like "I used 5 bags today")
- When you record usage, stock decreases automatically!
  - Had 50 bags
  - Used 5 bags
  - Now have 45 bags

### **Tasks: Pending vs Completed vs Overdue**
- **Pending** = Not done yet (still need to do it)
- **Completed** = Done! ✅
- **Overdue** = Past due date and still not done (needs attention! ⚠️)

### **Roles: Owner vs Manager vs Worker vs Viewer**
- **Owner**: You! Full control, can do everything
- **Manager**: Almost everything, but can't change billing or promote to Owner
- **Worker**: Can complete tasks, but can't see costs/prices
- **Viewer**: Can only look (read-only), can't change anything

## ❓ Common Questions

**Q: What if I make a mistake?**
A: Most things can be edited! Just go back to the item (expense, sale, task, etc.) and click "Edit". Some things (like processed payroll) can't be changed for accuracy, but you can add adjustments.

**Q: Can I use this offline?**
A: You need internet to save data, but you can view cached data offline. Always make sure you're online when recording important things!

**Q: How many farms can I manage?**
A: You can create multiple farms! Each farm has its own flocks, team, and data. Perfect if you have farms in different locations!

**Q: Can I export my data?**
A: Yes! You can export reports (PDF, Excel) from the Insights and Analytics pages. Your data is always yours!

**Q: What if my worker doesn't have a smartphone?**
A: They can still use the app on a computer at your farm. Or you can create tasks for them and they can mark them complete when you check.

**Q: How do I know if I'm making profit?**
A: Go to Insights page → See "Net Profit". 
- Positive number = Making money! ✅
- Negative number = Losing money ❌
- Check it weekly!

**Q: Can I track multiple types of animals?**
A: Currently, Edentrack is designed for poultry (chickens). But you could create separate farms for different animal types and use it creatively!

**Q: What if I forget to record something?**
A: You can add it later! Just make sure to use the correct date. For example, if you forgot to record yesterday's expense, enter it today but set the date to yesterday.

**Q: How do I know when chickens are ready to sell?**
A: Check the Weight Tracking page. The app tells you "Market Ready" when:
- Chickens are at least 6 weeks old (for broilers)
- Chickens weigh at least 2.0 kg
- Optimal weight is usually 2.5 kg (best profit time!)

**Q: Can multiple people use the app at the same time?**
A: Yes! Multiple people can use the app simultaneously. Data syncs in real-time, so everyone sees the latest updates!
